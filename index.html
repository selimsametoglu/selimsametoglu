I am a PhD candidate with a focus on leveraging data from social media and use this in genetically informative designs. I primarily make use of machine learning models (e.g., natural language processing, image to text, and cascaded/combined models) to assess wellbeing of individuals. I am also interested in situations where researchers only have limited access to labeled datasets (i.e., transfer learning). As of 2020, I have been working at The Netherlands Twin Register as a PhD candidate where I work on collecting and analyzing data from social media platforms.

Below here are some of my scientific contributions (either published or in progress)

  • The Value of Social Media Language for the Assessment of Wellbeing: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
  • Towards a better understanding of adolescent obsessive–compulsive personality traits and obsessive–compulsive symptoms from growth trajectories of perfectionism
  • Comparison of wellbeing networks based on social media language and survey responses
